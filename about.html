About Me

Here you will learn about my athletics, hobbies, and background.

Athletics

I am currently a Nationally ranked track and field athlete, with my main events being discus and shotput and dabbling in a bit of hammer. I compete with the Nanaimo Track and Field Club which is very handy since it is directly below VIU residences where I live. When I enjoy something, I find even more joy in mastering it as I do with programming, track is no exception. I powerlift 2-5 days a week and practice 2-3 days a week to improve myself. I also feel that all programmers should find some sport that they like, or physical activity. It is a good way to blow off steam, and keep your body active after sitting at a chair most of the day looking for a data leak!

Hobbies

To wind down after a long day of learning and going up an absurd amount of stairs to get to class, I have a couple things that I like to do. I play video-games. Like I play them a lot. I mentioned earlier that I touched a computer when I was 11, that was to play Minecraft and Overwatch, and I still play both of those games. I love games not only because of the communities, but also because I can get exactly what experience I want out of them. Long day? I can play minecraft and make and feed my axolotls that look like ponyo from the texture I downloaded. Need some adrenaline? Play Dota 2 and scream at my monitor when I die after teleporting into 5 people in a ranked game. On top of gaming, I also like to cook a lot. My favourite thing to make currently is bulgogi beef rice bowls with japanese barbecue mayo, so yummy!

Background

I was born on Saltspring Island which is pretty cool, if you like nature and are not afraid of grass (I am sometimes). Although it has its charm, there is not a whole lot to do other than exploring the wilderness; and I was not much of a wilderness explorer like Russel. I lived there and commuted over a ferry to school until 2016 where my family moved to Duncan where we had been commuting for school. I graduated in 2022 at 17 years old with Honours, and Student of Distinction awards. I was then accepted into VIU's Computer Science program and moved onto the Student Residences to start my first year of university. After the first year I was accepted to become a Community Leader on residences, which is my current occupation.
